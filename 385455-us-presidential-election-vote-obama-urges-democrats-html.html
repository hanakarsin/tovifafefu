    With campaigns and even preliminary voting already underway ahead of the United States presidential poll, former president Barack Obama has sent a vital message to Democrat voters.

    In a recent advert, the former US leader urged voters to join millions of Americans in the voting exercise so that the party can secure an early win and sustain it till the actual election.

    Obama advised the voters not to be reluctant to exercise their right, adding that the victory of the Democratic party depends a lot on their votes.

    He told Democrats should stop thinking the election is still far away when it is already on all over the country.

    The ex-US leader said:

    "The 2020 election isn't a few weeks away; it's already here. Millions of Americans are already voting; make sure you stand up and join them."It's going to be close. It could come down to a handful of voters just like you. So I'm asking you to bring this thing home. Leave no doubt. Vote early."

    The former president said being that there is a growing movement for justice, equality and progress on so many national issues, early voting is very pertinent.

    He said:

    "There will always be reasons to think your vote doesn't matter -- that's not new, what is new is a growing movement for justice, equality and progress on so many issues. This really is a tipping point, and that momentum only continues if we win this election."

    As earlier reported by TUKO.co.ke, Joe Biden, the US Democratic nominee for the November 3 presidential election, announced that the continuing fundraiser towards his White House bid raked in a record KSh 41.5 billion in September 2020.

    Biden made the announcement on Wednesday, October 14, alongside a "grassroots supporter" identified as Trimicka and described the donations as the "best month of fundraising in America's political history".

    The September donations were contributed by at least 5.5 million wellwishers who forked out an average of KSh 4,778 with online donors accounting for KSh 22 billion.

    Biden's campaign kitty now has a total of KSh 46.91 billion.
