    After aligning himself with a new political party, Kilifi Governor Amason Kingi has been removed as the county Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) party, chairman.

    Kingi’s replacement comes days after the Kilifi ODM youth league chairman Samir Nyundo resigned from the Orange party.

    Raila confirmed Kingi's ouster

    Nation reports that ODM party leader Raila Odinga confirmed that Kingi had been replaced by Ganze MP Teddy Mwambire, Kingi’s deputy chairman.

    Mwambire said he would take over the position but insisted that Kingi had not officially left ODM for his yet-to-be-launch party.

    “Governor Kingi has not officially left the party because he has not written to or consulted me. However, I can handle that position if he leaves the party because I hear he wants to form his own party,” Mwambire said.

    Mwambire also asked those wishing to leave the party to do it with immediate effect.

    Addressing the press on Monday, August 9, Kingi said that plans are underway to create a national outlook party.

    ''We want to have a national political party or rather a political formation that has its roots in the coastal region. This means that it will not only have candidates from the coastal region but across the country,'' Kingi said.

    Sharp divisions emerge over formation of the new party

    Differences over forming a coast regional political party continue to cause friction between Kingi and his Mombasa counterpart Hassan Joho.

    ODM officials in Kilifi had recently vowed to replace Kingi as county party chairman.

    This is after he came under harsh criticism for supporting a coalition by five coast-based parties last month.

    The new coalition dubbed the Coast Integrated Development Initiative and which Kingi has vowed to back, saying, ODM has done little to deliver development to the region, comprises Shirikisho Party of Kenya, Kadu-Asili, Republican Congress Party of Kenya, Umoja Summit Party of Kenya, and the Communist Party of Kenya.

    Kingi’s position has rekindled his fight with Joho, the ODM deputy party leader after the former skipped Raila’s recent tour of Kilifi county.

    Speaking separately, Mwambire said they are putting measures to popularise the party across the coastal region and make it stronger ahead of the 2022 General Election.

    ''If there are people who feel that the party is facing some challenges, they should consult with us to jointly know what to do about it. Anyone who feels they want to leave the party should do so that we can have room to prepare for the forthcoming polls,'' Mwambire explained.

    Uhuru has settled for Raila's candidacy

    As previously reported by TUKO.co.ke, Jubilee vice-chair David Murathe, said President Uhuru Kenyatta had settled on the former prime minister.

    Murathe confided that the Mombasa meeting would solely be about Uhuru's 2022 succession and the plan to hand Raila the lead in the upcoming vote.

    "Raila Odinga is on his way to Mombasa to meet President Uhuru Kenyatta. I had said before, prepare for Jakom to be the fifth President," Murathe said.

    With the likelihood of Raila's ODM joining forces with Jubilee, Murathe said the resolution by the opposition party to ditch the NASA coalition was opportune, adding that ODM would now be free to coalesce with the ruling party for 2022 purposes.
