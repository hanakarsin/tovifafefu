    Senior magistrate arrested for allegedly killing her husband in cold blood

    -Pauline Omungala Maisy Chesang is expected to be charged with the murder of her lawyer husband which took place in February -She is the third top judicial officer to be arrested and charged with a high-profile crime in recent months.

    15 Feb 2024, 00:00

    -Pauline Omungala Maisy Chesang is expected to be charged with the murder of her lawyer husband which took place in February

    -She is the third top judicial officer to be arrested and charged with a high-profile crime in recent months.

    -DCJ Philomena Mwilu was arrested in August 2018 and is facing graft charges before the Anti-Corruption Court

    -Lawyer Tom Ojienda , a JSC member, was in December 2018 arrested over a KSh 89 million fraud claim

    A serving magistrate has been arrested in connection with the murder of her lawyer husband at their home in Machakos county on February 17, 2019.

    Nyeri senior principal magistrate, Pauline Omungala, was on Sunday, March 3, summoned by Athi River police officers investigating the murder of Robert Chesang' and locked up as a suspect.

    Her arrest brings to five the number of suspects arrested in connection with the murder which the detectives described as an assassination.

    Lawyer Chesang’ was shot seven times in broad daylight at their posh home in Machakos.

    Omugala’s arrest brings to three the number of top judicial officers arrested in relation to various high-profile crimes in recent months.

    In August 2018, Deputy Chief Justice Philomena Mwilu was arrested and arraigned before the Anti-Corruption Court.

    TUKO.co.ke understands the DCJ is facing more than 12 charges, among them corruptly receiving KSh 12 million from the collapsed Imperial Bank.

    In December 2018, city lawyer Tom Ojienda was arrested and temporarily locked up over corruption claims.

    The senior counsel, who sits in the Judicial Service Commission (JSC), was arraigned after it was alleged he pocketed a sum of over KSh 89 million ‘fake legal fees' Mumias Sugar Company.

    Officers drawn from the Special Crimes Prevention Unit and Flying Squad were at the time also hunting down a High Court judge in relation to the matter.

    Ojienda got a reprieve after High Court Judge Chacha Mwita stopped his criminal prosecution, sparking public outcry as the ruling was widely seen as biased in the lawyer’s favour.

    In Omugala’s case, detectives say the life between the magistrate and her late husband had been characterised by domestic violence occasioned by fights over property. This found its way into courts and it is believed that being a magistrate, Omugala was grossly embarrassed by the cases leaking to the public domain.

    In one of the cases, the late Chesang’ sought the court’s protection against domestic violence.

    The deceased had reported that he had been forced to move out of his house because his wife was violent and was using police officers to harass and intimidate him.

    He requested the court to grant him unconditional access to his home.

    Chesang claimed the locks to the home had been changed and the house was under police guard.

    He also said his magistrate wife had transferred his two children to a new school and denied him access to them.

    “From August 17 to date, I’ve lost all that I’ve acquired progressively since I started married life due to illegal actions of the respondents. I have technically lost the house, the cars and all my earthly belongings,” Chesang’ said in an affidavit.

    He also asked the court to allow him access to his two vehicles, a Lexus and Premio, which he claimed were being used by police officers.

    Interestingly, among the suspects already arrested in relation to the murder include two serving police officers and a retired officer.

    Two officers attached to Mutuine Police Station in Dagoretti, Nairobi, came under scrutiny after it emerged that the car used to ferry the killers to Chesang’s house in Lukenya belonged to one of them.

    In the court proceedings, Chesang had further expressed dissatisfaction with the manner in which his case was being handled, alleging interference from his wife and other magistrates.

    On September 24, 2018, he, for instance, wrote that while seated at a different table, senior resident magistrate Linus Kassan loudly discussed the case on the phone.

    His case had been transferred five times before a ruling was made.
