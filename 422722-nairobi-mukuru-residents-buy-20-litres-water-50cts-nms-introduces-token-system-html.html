    Residents of Mukuru are set to buy 20 litres of water at 50 cents after the Nairobi Metropolitan Services (NMS), and Nairobi City Water and Sewerage Company (NCWS) introduced a water token system.

    Water shortage in Mukuru Kwa Reuben

    Ten stations have already been installed at strategic points across the sprawling informal settlement, with Mukuru Kwa Reuben set to be the project's first beneficiary.

    According to NMS Deputy Director for Water and Sanitation Stephen Githinji, the project will help the residents to access clean water at an affordable price to the low-income.

    Githinji stated that with this system, residents will only be required to purchase water tokens depending on the amount of water they require.

    “Once residents are given the chip, they will insert it into a gadget at the water stations which will automatically release the amount of water the resident requested for,” said Githinji.

    Githinji said with the token system, the residents will not need too many people to man the water station since they are connected to Nairobi Water and NMS for remote monitoring and supervision.

    “With the water token system, we don’t need many people to man the station. Only one officer will be in charge to serve the consumers,” he said.

    Mukuru slums host almost half a million people and are divided into five areas, including Kwa Njenga, Kwa Reuben, Fuata Nyayo, Pipeline and Viwandani.

    The residents have had to make long queues to access free water distributed daily by NMS, with one million litres of water supplied across the city's informal settlements every day using 42 water browsers.

    In a separate story, TUKO.co.ke reported that the Nairobi Water and Sewerage Company had listed over 10 places in the city that will face water supply interruption from Thursday, May 6, at 6:00am to Friday, May 7, at 6:00am.

    In a statement issued on May 4, the water supplier said State House and other crucial government departments would be affected by the interruption occasioned by repairing the Sasumua treated water transmission pipeline located in Aberdare Forest.
