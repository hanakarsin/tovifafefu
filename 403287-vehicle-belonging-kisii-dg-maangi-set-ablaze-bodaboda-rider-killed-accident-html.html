    Vehicle belonging to Kisii DG Maangi set ablaze after bodaboda rider was killed in accident

    - Kisii deputy governor Joash Maangi was involved in a car accident that killed two people - The deputy governor took off from the scene right before his official vehicle was burnt to ashes - Maangi, his driver and bodyguard escaped unhurt in the grisly road accident

    27 May 2024, 00:00

    - Kisii deputy governor Joash Maangi was involved in a car accident that killed two people

    - The deputy governor took off from the scene right before his official vehicle was burnt to ashes

    - Maangi, his driver and bodyguard escaped unhurt in the grisly road accident

    Kisii deputy governor Joash Maangi was involved in a road accident on Wednesday at around 7:30pm in Olulung’a along the Narok-Nairobi Highway.

    Fortunately, the deputy governor and his driver and bodyguard who were accompanying him back to Nairobi were not harmed in the accident which killed two, a bodaboda rider and his passenger.

    “My driver was driving my official car as we were on our way to Nairobi when the bodaboda operator abruptly joined the road from a bushy path and quickly turned towards us hitting the car head on,” said Maangi.

    Angry residents of Olulung’a reportedly took matters in their own hands and set the vehicle ablaze when they discovered that a bodaboda rider had been killed in accident involving the deputy governor's car.

    Despite plea of the police officers who were trying to contain the situation, the residents still chose to burn Maangi's car in revenge for the death of the rider.

    The DG was able to flee the scene in a good Samaritan’s vehicle moments before the angry mob set his vehicle on fire.

    The police in Narok are currently investigating the incident.

    A day before the accident involving Maangi, the deputy governor alongside South Mugurango Member of Parliament Sylvanus Osoro threatened to sue the government for harassments by state agents.

    While addressing the residents of South Mugirango after their release, the two allies of Deputy President William Ruto claimed they were being persecuted for their association with the second-in-command.

    "I am telling you people, members of the security team, specifically those who arrested me...I am going to file a case against you. I am seeking damages...You must pay for the torture I underwent inside the cells. Intimidation and threats won't work," said Osoro.
