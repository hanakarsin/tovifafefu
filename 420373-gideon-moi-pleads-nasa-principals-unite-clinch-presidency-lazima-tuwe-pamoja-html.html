    KANU party chairman Gideon Moi has pleaded with National Super Alliance (NASA) principals to unite and work together ahead of the 2022 General Election.

    Addressing KANU delegates from Kirinyaga at Kabarnet Gardens, Nairobi on Wednesday, July 28, the Baringo senator asked his fellow party leaders to join hands amid NASA coalition fallout.

    "Musalia Mudavadi, Kalonzo Musyoka, Moses Wetang'ula and Raila Odinga, if we work together and support the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI), and inclusivity for all then as we push for the prosperity of the nation we must be together," he said.

    NASA split

    Signs that all is not well in the Opposition were displayed when Wiper and Ford Kenya led by Kalonzo and Wetang'ula respectively initiated a process to pull out of NASA.

    Kalonzo maintained Wiper would not engage nor join forces with NASA n its State House journey and instead vouched for the One Kenya Alliance.

    Addressing journalists at the party headquarters on Monday, July 26, the former vice president announced he had officially cut ties with the 2017 NASA coalition.

    Speaking to Citizen TV on the morning of Tuesday, July 6, Ford Kenya acting secretary-general Chris Wamalwa accused the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) top leadership of sidelining its other partners in the coalition.

    'Our big brothers ODM have betrayed us. They got the funds, but when we tried to engage them, they refused to share the funds with us. One of the agreements in NASA is when the three parties pull out, then NASA will be dead,'' Wamalwa said.

    Uhuru challenges NASA principals to unite

    President Uhuru Kenyatta previously declared that he will pick his 2022 successor from among the NASA principals if they unite and agree on a single candidate.

    The declaration knocks off Deputy President William Ruto from Uhuru’s succession matrix despite his first term assurance that he would support him in his famous 'yangu, Ruto kumi' quote.

    For the first time, the head of state challenged the NASA principals to unite to enhance their chances of forming the next government after his second and last term comes to an end in 2022.

    Uhuru and Ruto have not been seeing eye to eye during their second term in office, with the head of state giving signals that he was politically done with his deputy.
