    The move by the Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i to deport 17 betting firms directors from the country has received applause from former Kakamega senator Boni Khalwale.

    According to the former senator, the move by Matiang'i will streamline the gaming industry which he claimed had transformed the country to the level of popular American city, Las Vegas.

    While taking to Twitter to express his gratitude to the CS, Khalwale further cautioned the 17 directors should exit the country quietly.

    " Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i deserves golden accolades for his efforts on streamlining the betting industry," said the former senator."The 17 betting directors facing deportation must exist quietly. Transforming Kenya into a Las Vegas of Africa is a luxury we can ill afford," he added.

    READ ALSO: Woman narrates how she accidentally slept with brother-in-law thinking he was her hubby

    Khalwale's remarks came hours after Matiangi signed deportation orders for the officials, mainly from Eastern Europe, who were accused of violating their work permits to engage in gambling.

    “Most East Europeans violated work permits by engaging in business not stated on applications, the deportation orders are immediate," the ministry announced.

    The matching orders were issued after the National Security Advisory Committee (NSAC), decided there would be no backing down on the war against rogue betting companies.

    The identities of the targeted directors or that of their firms were not disclosed.

    “This is still an ongoing operation so we won’t disclose their names for now because those being deported have to be arrested first,” added the ministry.

    This followed an intensified crackdown by the government on gambling and it even ordered mobile telecommunication companies to shut down short codes operated by betting firms.

    But President Uhuru Kenyatta vowed that his government would not relent on the war against gambling in the country, leading to Tuesday’s deportations.

    A total of 27 firms were blacklisted by the Betting Licence and Control Board (BLCB) on grounds that they failed to comply with the set regulations.

    Betin Kenya which is among the companies whose licenses were not renewed threatened to take the matter to court, maintaining it had complied with all the regulations, adding the suspension was malicious.
