    Moody Awori Gets Orders to Repossess Late Brother's 30-Acre Land from Trespasser

    Former vice president Moody Awori can breathe a sigh of relief after he managed to evict a trespasser on a parcel of land owned by his late brother Hannington Awori.     Former vice president Moody Awori can breathe a sigh of relief after he managed to evict a trespasser on a parcel of land owned by his late brother Hannington Awori.

    Environment and Land Court judge Justice Anne Omollo acknowledged that Moody, 92, proved his case against Charles Onyango Omondi to warrant the eviction.

    Omondi claimed ownership of the controversial piece of land for 10 years.

    "In light of the evidence adduced, I am satisfied that Awori has proved his claim against Omondi for eviction only," ruled the judge.

    Moody sued Omondi on behalf of his sister-in-law

    The former VP sued Omondi in October 2016 on behalf of his sister-in-law Pamela Mary Awori.

    The court heard that Pamela is the registered owner of the land - Samia/Wakhungu/Odiado/589.

    Omondi was accused of legally taking over the parcel of land.

    The court heard that some of the buildings rented to third parties were a bakery.

    Pamela inherited the parcel after her husband Hannington, a famed corporate executive of Kenya's pioneer civil engineers, died.

    Pamela gave Moody the right to prosecute the case

    The Standard reports that Pamela gave Moody rights to prosecute the case on her behalf under a power of attorney, a legal procedure that allows Kenyans to pick anyone to represent them in court.

    "It is without a doubt that the accused (Omondi) took advantage of the absence of my late brother's family and moved into the property where he started renting out the premises to third parties,'' Moody said.

    But in his defence, the accused told the court that on June 5, 2010, a son of Moody's brother Jeremy Hannington Edward Awori, leased the parcel and buildings to him for ten years at an agreed price KSh 1.2 million, which he paid in full.

    However, during the hearing of the case, Omondi was at pains to produce receipts to back the claim.

    The court said that he engaged in the repair works without the actual owner of the land that had been developed.

    The death of Aggrey Awori

    On Monday, July 5, TUKO.co.ke reported the death of Aggrey Awori, 82, the younger brother of the former vice president.

    Aggrey died after a month-long illness, his close friend pointing at pressure as the cause of the death.

    "He has been sick for about a month and I have been in touch with him until today when his wife, Thelma told me that he had died," said Peter Oguttu, one of Aggrey's closest friends.

    Oguttu stated COVID-19 might have been another cause, given the deceased had other underlying conditions.
