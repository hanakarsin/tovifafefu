    Mohammed Bashir: High-Profile Life of Slain Businessman Who Walked with High and Mighty

    - Slain American businessman Mohammed Bashir was a well-connected person who worked with senior government officials - In October 2020, he accompanied President Uhuru to the inspection of Uhuru Business Park in Kisumu

    07 May 2024, 00:00

    - Slain American businessman Mohammed Bashir was a well-connected person who worked with senior government officials

    - In October 2020, he accompanied President Uhuru to the inspection of Uhuru Business Park in Kisumu

    - In Somalia, his development company constructed the presidential palace, Somali Villa and the Ministry of Health's headquarters

    On Saturday, May 22, the family of Somali-American businessman Mohammed Bashir who had been missing for 10 days announced they had found him dead.

    Bashir was last seen leaving Mialle Lounge in Lavington, Nairobi on Thursday, May 13, and days later, his burnt car was found in Kajiado raising fears of his whereabouts.

    According to a report by Daily Nation, the 35-year-old was a well-connected businessman and through his Infinity Development company, he had landed himself multi-billion deals with the Kenyan government.

    In October 2020, Bashir was pictured accompanying President Uhuru Kenyatta in an inspection tour of the Uhuru Business Park in Kisumu ahead of its reopening.

    Additionally, his firm was also part of the successful construction of the 21-storey Huru Towers in Westlands, West-Riverside Towers among other posh residential properties in the country.

    Similarly, in Somalia, Bashir worked with the senior government officials and his company constructed the magnificent Somalia presidential palace and the country's Ministry of Health headquarter buildings.

    Murder most foul

    On Saturday, the deceased's family positively identified his body which had been lying at the Kerugoya Level Five Hospital since May 15, a couple of days after he was last seen alive.

    Reports indicated his body had deep panga cuts and the Kirinyaga Police boss said he was seemingly tortured before he was killed.

    "His neck appeared to have been snapped and there are scars on his chest that made it look like he had been dragged," his lawyers said.

    The mystery surrounding his abduction and subsequent gruesome murder raises questions on who wanted Bashir dead and whether was indeed a security threat bearing in mind he had gone close enough to the head of state and other government officials.

    On Sunday, May 23, his family dismissed claims that the businessman was linked to terror and money laundering as some reports had suggested.

    “The transactions Bashir was involved in were straightforward and did not have any illegalities,” family lawyer Alibaya Hassan said.
