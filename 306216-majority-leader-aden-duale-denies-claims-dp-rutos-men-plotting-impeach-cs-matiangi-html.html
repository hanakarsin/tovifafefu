    Majority leader Aden Duale denies claims DP Ruto's men plotting to impeach CS Matiang'i

    - Duale said as the Majority leader, he was not aware of any motion seeking to remove Matiang'i from office - The legislator said in the event there was such a plan, then he would come out to defend the Interior Cabinet Secretary

    06 Aug 2024, 00:00

    - Duale said as the Majority leader, he was not aware of any motion seeking to remove Matiang'i from office

    - The legislator said in the event there was such a plan, then he would come out to defend the Interior Cabinet Secretary

    - The Garissa Town MP warned removing a State officer in the position of a CS was not a walk in the park

    - He was reacting to claims by Minority leader Mbadi who had alleged MPs allied to DP Ruto were planning to impeach Matiang'i

    National Assembly Majority leader Aden Duale has rubbished claims that MPs allied to Deputy President William Ruto are planning to eject Interior Cabinet Secretary (CS) Fred Matiang'i from office.

    Duale who doubles as Jubilee party's official spokesperson clarified removal of a Cabinet secretary or any other State officer must follow strict procedures put in place by the ruling party and meet the required threshold.

    READ ALSO: Gavana wa Trans Nzoia Patrick Khaemba motoni baada ya kumpigia DP Ruto debe

    Addressing the press on Wednesday, May 22, at Parliament buildings in Nairobi, the Garissa Town MP assured the public as the Majority leader, he was not privy to any motion or plot seeking to impeach Matiang'i.

    “Nobody has initiated a motion of removal from office of Interior CS Dr Fred Matiang’i. And If it ever came, I would be the first to defend the government official,” Duale said.

    READ ALSO: Governor Waititu claims Raila is a beneficiary of fake gold scandal involving Dubai royals

    He was reacting to National Assembly's Minority leader John Mbadi who sensationally claimed members of the Tanga Tanga team lead by Ruto were working on a motion to remove Matiang'i from office.

    The Suba South MP made the allegations after the Tanga Tanga group called on the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) to investigate the CS and Opposition leader Raila Odinga whose names had come up in the multi-billion fake gold scam.

    "The deputy president and his team have refused to allow the Directorate of Criminal Investigation (DCI) to their work and have resorted to playing politics aimed at removing one Fred Matiang'i from office on grounds that he protected the suspected fake gold dealers," Mbadi told journalists on May 20.

    READ ALSO: ODM, Jubilee MPs embroiled in ugly spat over who between DP Ruto and Raila is conman

    The ODM party national chairman further alleged Ruto had been unhappy ever since Matiang'i was elevated to the position of being a supervisor and coordinator of all national government's development projects and has been looking for an opportunity to fight back.

    "Since Matiang'i was added the responsibility of coordinating national functions, the deputy president has been very unhappy and together with his team think they have found an opportunity to fight back," the Minority leader claimed.

    Just like Duale, Mbadi also vowed to defend the CS against the forces seeking to terminate his career.

    "Let them bring that motion, we will deal with it accordingly," he said.
