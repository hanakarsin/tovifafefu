    Ever since the enactment of the new education reforms, it has become a norm that KCSE candidates get to receive their examination results before celebrating Christmas. KCSE 2019 results were released by Professor George Magoha on 18th December, which was three days earlier than the release date in 2018. How was the performance of the school per county?

    Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) examination results for the year 2019 were released on Wednesday evening by the Cabinet Secretary of Education, Professor George Magoha, at the KNEC headquarters. The total number of candidates who sat for this year's exams was 699, 745 in the 47 counties.

    Top schools in KCSE 2019 per county

    The ranking of schools was scraped off by the former CS of Education, Fred Matiangi, during the various reforms he enacted in the education sector. Therefore, this means that KCSE results 2019 school ranking is not an activity that is carried out by the Ministry of Education. However, with the information on the top sub-county schools as well as the top extra county KCSE schools, it is easy to come up with the list of top schools per county.
