    Farming seems to be a booming business not only in Kenya but in the entire world.

    Many people have been engaging in farming either for profit or for passion and love for farming.

    As a matter of fact, Facebook CEO Mark Zuckerberg recently disclosed to the world he is a good farmer and actually provided proof.

    In a Facebook post seen by TUKO.co.ke, Zuckerberg revealed to fans that he is an animal farmer who also deals with goat rearing.

    The Facebook CEO shared a photo of two of his goats at his farm followed by a short introduction of their names.

    In his caption, Zuckerberg noted his goats go by the name Max and Bitcoin.

    ''My goats: Max and Bitcoin," Zuckerberg wrote.

    The post which was shared on Monday, May 10, the afternoon had garnered 184,000 comments and over 18k shares by Tuesday, May 11, afternoon.

    Most people who commented on the post also shared photos of themselves with their goats.

    Zuckerberg's post came barely a month after the CEO sent a message of solidarity to the bereaved family of George Floyd, who was killed by a US ex-police officer, Derek Chauvin.

    TUKO.co.ke reported how Zuckerberg in a solemn tone on Tuesday, April 20, expressed hope that the eventual murder charge levied on Chauvin will bring a measure of comfort to late Floyd's family and friends.

    He noted that the court's decision is part of a bigger struggle against racism and injustice which is rampant in the country.

    "Right now I'm thinking of George Floyd, his family, and those who knew him. I hope this verdict brings some measure of comfort to them, and to everyone who can't help but see themselves in his story. We stand in solidarity with you, knowing that this is part of a bigger struggle against racism and injustice," he shared on his Facebook page.
