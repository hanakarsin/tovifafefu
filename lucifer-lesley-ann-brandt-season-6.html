    Lesley-Ann Brandt Reveals Maze's Final Scene With Lucifer Was Rewritten, Per Request (EXCLUSIVE)

    Lesley-Ann Brandt spoke exclusively with 'Distractify' about Season 6 of 'Lucifer' and revealed that she asked the writers to change Maze's final scene. Our favorite devilish show has finally returned...

    17 Mar 2024, 00:00

    Lesley-Ann Brandt spoke exclusively with 'Distractify' about Season 6 of 'Lucifer' and revealed that she asked the writers to change Maze's final scene.

    Our favorite devilish show has finally returned...

    The first half of Season 5 of the hit series Lucifer left fans on a major cliffhanger when God finally took human form and broke up a fight between Lucifer, Michael, and Amenadiel. With so many questions left unanswered, fans had to wait a whole year before the second half of Season 5 dropped on Netflix (May 28).

    Ahead of Season 5B, Distractify spoke exclusively with Lesley-Ann Brandt — who plays the demon Mazikeen — about her character's journey throughout the series, and what fans can expect from the total "badass" in Season 6.

    The actress also revealed to us that she was not content with Maze's original ending. She worked with Lucifer writer Chris Rafferty to find a "happy medium." Keep reading to find out more.

    Lesley-Ann Brandt spoke with 'Lucifer' writers about changing Maze's ending in Season 6.

    [Warning: Spoilers ahead]

    Does Maze die in Season 5B?

    Fans of the comic book character can breathe easy, as Maze only pretends to die to help Lucifer pull a prank on Dan. When the demon jumps in front of a bullet to save Dan's life, she fakes her own death, but soon gets up and starts laughing.

    While we're happy to know that Maze is safe, in typical Lucifer form, the series left audiences on another major cliffhanger. Since Season 6 has already finished filming, Distractify spoke with Lesley-Ann about wrapping up the hit series.

    Though the South African native didn't reveal any plot spoilers, she did tell us that she worked with Lucifer writers to change her character's original ending.

    "The final scene that Lucifer and Mazikeen have in the whole series, the way it was originally written was with a lot of thought, but what I felt was, it kind of wasn’t paying homage to this relationship and this friendship," she told us. "I was craving a deeper version of it."

    The Netflix star told us that she called up the writer and had a "beautiful discussion" about the character and eventually found a "happy medium" on Maze's ending.

    "It’s one of the most painful scenes for the character of Maze but one of the most fulfilling as the actress playing her," she continued. "It’s not a huge, huge scene but it’s so impactful and there’s so much that’s being said in between the lines, between these two, and I'm really looking forward to seeing that myself. Hopefully it will resonate with the audience as well."

    The 'Lucifer' star opened up about the "emotional" Season 6 ending.

    It's the final curtain call for Lucifer fans.

    Although Season 5B of the show just dropped on Netflix, the cast and crew already know each characters' fate as the final season wrapped on shooting earlier this year.

    "It’s terribly emotional," Lesley-Ann said. "I am happy with where the show finishes and I think everyone else is sort of happy for where their characters finish. I haven’t heard otherwise."

    The actress teased that Season 6 will bring in some elements from the comic book too.

    "I think we found this beautiful way to honor the show we created," she told Distractify. "I think by the end of the series, [fans will] really sit back and go, ‘Wow that was a great 6 years. Let me binge it from the beginning.’"
